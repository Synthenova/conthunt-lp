# How to Upload a YouTube Short: Step-by-Step Guide for 2026

Uploading YouTube Shorts is one of the first skills every creator needs to master. Whether you're recording directly in the app or uploading pre-edited content from your computer, getting the upload process right is essential for your content to be discovered.

In this guide, I'll walk you through every method for uploading YouTube Shorts, along with tips to ensure your content performs well.

## YouTube Shorts Upload Requirements

Before uploading, make sure your video meets these specifications:

| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|----------------|
| **Aspect Ratio** | 9:16 (vertical) |
| **Resolution** | 720p (1080x1920 recommended) |
| **Duration** | 1 second to 60 seconds |
| **File Format** | MOV, MP4, MPEG, WebM |
| **File Size** | Up to 256 GB |
| **Frame Rate** | 24, 25, 30, 48, 50, or 60 FPS |

> **Important**: If your video is horizontal (16:9) or longer than 60 seconds, YouTube will categorize it as a regular video, not a Short.

## Method 1: Upload from YouTube Mobile App

The mobile app is the fastest way to upload Shorts directly from your phone.

### Step-by-Step: Mobile Upload

**Step 1: Open YouTube App**
Tap the **+** button at the bottom center of the screen

**Step 2: Select "Create a Short"**
This opens the Shorts recording interface

**Step 3: Choose Your Video Source**
- **Record new**: Hold the red record button
- **Upload from gallery**: Tap the gallery icon in the bottom left

**Step 4: Trim Your Video (if needed)**
- Drag the sliders to set start and end points
- Preview your trimmed video
- Tap "Next" when satisfied

**Step 5: Add Enhancements**
- **Music**: Add from YouTube's audio library
- **Text**: Overlay text with timing controls
- **Filters**: Adjust visual appearance
- **Timer**: Set countdown for recording

**Step 6: Add Video Details**
- **Title**: Required, up to 100 characters
- **Description**: Add context and links
- **Settings**: Visibility, comments, etc.

**Step 7: Upload**
- Tap "Post Short" for immediate publishing
- Or schedule for a specific date/time

## Troubleshooting Upload Issues

### Issue 1: Video Not Showing as Short

**Causes:**
- Video is horizontal (not 9:16 aspect ratio)
- Video is longer than 60 seconds
- Missing #shorts hashtag

**Solutions:**
1. Verify video is vertical (9:16)
2. Trim video to under 60 seconds
3. Add #shorts to title or description
4. Wait 24-48 hours for indexing

### Issue 2: Upload Fails or Stalls

**Solutions:**
1. Check your internet connection
2. Try a different browser or device
3. Clear cache and cookies
4. Reduce file size if very large
5. Ensure file format is supported

### Issue 3: Poor Video Quality

**Causes:**
- Low original resolution
- Excessive compression
- Wrong aspect ratio

**Solutions:**
1. Export at 1080x1920 resolution
2. Use higher bitrate when exporting
3. Verify 9:16 aspect ratio before upload
4. Check YouTube Studio processing is complete

### Issue 4: Copyright Claims

**Solutions:**
1. Use royalty-free music from YouTube Audio Library
2. Remove or replace copyrighted content
3. Accept claim if you have permission
4. Dispute if you believe claim is incorrect

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I upload horizontal video as a Short?

Technically yes, but it won't perform well. YouTube may still categorize it as a Short if under 60 seconds with #shorts, but vertical format is strongly recommended.

### Why is my Short blurry after upload?

This usually happens if the original resolution is too low or YouTube is still processing. Wait 1-2 hours for full processing. If still blurry, the issue is likely the source video quality.

### Can I edit a Short after uploading?

You can edit the title, description, and settings after uploading. You cannot edit the video content itself without deleting and re-uploading.

### What's the maximum file size for Shorts?

YouTube allows files up to 256 GB, but Shorts are typically much smaller. A 60-second 1080p Short is usually 50-200 MB depending on content complexity.

### Should I add captions to my Shorts?

Yes! Captions improve accessibility and engagement. Many viewers watch without sound, and captions help with understanding. YouTube can auto-generate captions, but manual editing improves accuracy.

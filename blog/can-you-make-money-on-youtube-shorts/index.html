Can You Make Money on YouTube Shorts? Complete 2026 Guide

The short answer is yes, you can make money on YouTube Shorts—but the path to profitability might look different than you expect. While direct ad revenue from Shorts is modest, successful creators have built six-figure incomes through strategic monetization approaches.

In this guide, we’ll break down every revenue stream available to Shorts creators and show you how to build a sustainable income from short-form content.

Quick Overview: How Shorts Creators Make Money

Monetization Method Income Potential Difficulty
YouTube Ad Revenue $10-$500/month Easy (after qualifying)
Brand Sponsorships $500-$10,000+/deal Medium
Affiliate Marketing 5-30% commission Easy
Memberships & Donations $100-$5000/month Medium
Product Sales Unlimited Hard
Funneling to Long-Form Indirect revenue Medium

YouTube Shorts Ad Revenue: The Basics

YouTube Shorts generates ad revenue through ads that play between videos in the Shorts feed. Here’s what you need to know:

YouTube Partner Program Requirements for Shorts

To qualify for ad revenue from Shorts, you need to meet one of these thresholds:

Option A: Shorts-Specific Path

  • 10 million valid public Shorts views in the past 90 days

Option B: Traditional Path

  • 1,000 subscribers
  • 4,000 public watch hours in the past 12 months

Once you qualify and join the YouTube Partner Program, you’ll earn revenue from both your Shorts and long-form content.

How Much Does YouTube Pay for Shorts?

YouTube Shorts RPM (Revenue Per 1,000 views) typically ranges from $0.01 to $0.08. This means:

Monthly Views Estimated Earnings
100,000 $1 - $8
500,000 $5 - $40
1,000,000 $10 - $80
5,000,000 $50 - $400
10,000,000 $100 - $800

While these numbers may seem modest, many creators use Shorts to build audiences that then monetize through more lucrative long-form videos.

Important: Use analytics tools to track your Shorts performance and identify which content drives the most growth and revenue potential.

Brand Sponsorships: Where the Real Money Is

Brand deals are often the most lucrative revenue stream for Shorts creators. Here’s why brands invest in Shorts:

  • High engagement: Shorts average 60%+ view-through rates
  • Authentic reach: Creators have trusted relationships with audiences
  • Targeted exposure: Niche creators deliver focused audiences

Typical Brand Deal Rates for Shorts

Follower Count Rate per Short Rate per Month (Multiple Shorts)
10,000 $100 - $300 $500 - $1,000
50,000 $300 - $800 $1,500 - $3,000
100,000 $500 - $1,500 $2,500 - $5,000
500,000+ $2,000 - $10,000+ $10,000+

Types of Brand Deals for Shorts Creators

  1. Native Integration: Product mentions in your content style
  2. Dedicated Short: Full video showcasing a product
  3. Content Series: Multiple Shorts over a campaign period
  4. Affiliate Partnership: Commission-based ongoing relationship

Finding Brand Sponsorships

  • Direct outreach: Contact brands you already use and love
  • Creator marketplaces: Join platforms like Aspire, Grapevine, or Candid
  • Social DMs: Many brands discover creators through Instagram and TikTok
  • In your bio: Add a business email for partnership inquiries

Affiliate Marketing: Passive Income from Shorts

Affiliate marketing lets you earn commissions by recommending products in your Shorts content.

How Affiliate Marketing Works on Shorts

  1. Join affiliate programs (Amazon Associates, ShareASale, individual brands)
  2. Create content featuring products naturally
  3. Include affiliate links in your video description
  4. Earn commissions on qualifying purchases

Best Affiliate Categories for Shorts Creators

Category Why It Works Commission Range
Tech & Gadgets Visual demonstrations work well 3-10%
Beauty & Makeup Tutorial format natural fit 5-20%
Fashion & Apparel Outfit inspiration content 5-15%
Software & Apps Digital products easy to promote 20-50%
Fitness Equipment Exercise demos create demand 5-15%

Affiliate Strategy Tips

  • Create value-first content: Don’t just sell, teach or entertain
  • Use tracking links: Monitor which products convert best
  • Disclose affiliate relationships: Required by FTC and YouTube
  • Focus on relevant products: Promote things your audience actually wants

YouTube Memberships: Recurring Monthly Income

Once you reach 500 subscribers, you can enable channel memberships—giving fans a way to support you monthly in exchange for exclusive perks.

Membership Tier Ideas for Shorts Creators

Tier Price Perks to Offer
Basic $0.99/month Exclusive badges, community posts
Standard $4.99/month Early access, behind-the-scenes content
Premium $9.99/month Custom emojis, exclusive Shorts, Q&A access

What Makes Memberships Work for Shorts

  • Consistent posting: Members expect regular content
  • Exclusive value: Give members something non-members don’t get
  • Community building: Make members feel like insiders
  • Recognition: Highlight members in your content when appropriate

Using Shorts to Drive Long-Form Revenue

One of the most effective monetization strategies is using Shorts to funnel viewers to your long-form content, where RPM is significantly higher ($2-$10 per 1000 views vs. $0.01-$0.08 for Shorts).

The Shorts Funnel Strategy

Shorts (High Growth, Low RPM)
    ↓
Direct viewers to long-form videos
    ↓
Long-form Content (Moderate Growth, High RPM)
    ↓
Monetize with ads + memberships

Tactics to Drive Long-Form Views

  1. “Part 2” content: Create a Short that references a longer video
  2. Teaser content: Show highlights that make viewers want the full version
  3. Direct CTAs: Ask viewers to visit your channel for the full story
  4. Pinned comments: Use the first comment to link to related long-form content

Selling Your Own Products or Services

Shorts can be an excellent marketing tool for your own offerings:

Digital Products

  • Online courses: $50-$500 per sale
  • E-books and guides: $10-$50 per sale
  • Presets and templates: $10-$100 per sale
  • Coaching/consulting: $50-$500 per hour

Physical Products

  • Merchandise: T-shirts, hoodies, accessories
  • Custom products: Art, crafts, or niche items
  • Branded gear: Build your personal brand

Using Shorts for Product Promotion

  • Show, don’t tell: Demonstrate products in action
  • User-generated content: Feature customers using your products
  • Limited offers: Create urgency with time-sensitive deals
  • Behind-the-scenes: Show product development or creation

Alternative Monetization Platforms

Don’t limit yourself to YouTube—repurpose your Shorts for additional revenue:

TikTok Creator Fund

  • Monetize the same content on TikTok
  • Requirements: 10,000 followers, 100,000 video views in 30 days
  • Earnings vary widely based on views and engagement

Instagram Reels Bonuses

  • Instagram occasionally pays for Reels performance
  • Invite-only, focus on high-engagement content
  • Can earn $100-$10,000+ per month when eligible

Brand Sponsorships Across Platforms

  • Multi-platform presence increases your value to sponsors
  • Bundle packages across YouTube, TikTok, and Instagram
  • Demonstrate cross-platform reach

Frequently Asked Questions

How many views do you need to make money on YouTube Shorts?

For ad revenue, you need 10 million public Shorts views in 90 days OR 1,000 subscribers with 4,000 watch hours. For brand deals, creators can start earning with as few as 10,000 followers.

Do YouTube Shorts pay as well as long videos?

No. Shorts RPM ($0.01-$0.08) is much lower than long-form ($2-$10). However, Shorts grow audiences faster and can lead to other revenue streams.

Can you monetize Shorts without being in the YouTube Partner Program?

Yes! You can earn through brand sponsorships, affiliate marketing, and selling your own products without being in YPP. However, you need YPP for ad revenue and memberships.

Is it worth making YouTube Shorts for money?

Yes, if you have a comprehensive monetization strategy. Relying solely on ad revenue from Shorts is difficult, but combining multiple income streams makes Shorts profitable for many creators.

Bottom Line

Yes, you can make money on YouTube Shorts—but the most successful creators don’t rely on ad revenue alone. By combining multiple monetization streams (ad revenue, brand deals, affiliate marketing, memberships, and product sales), you can build a sustainable income from short-form content.

Start by focusing on creating valuable, engaging content that attracts a loyal audience. Then, layer in monetization methods that make sense for your niche and audience. Use tools like ContHunt to track your performance and optimize your content strategy based on real data.

The key is patience: Most successful Shorts creators took 6-12 months of consistent posting before reaching significant income levels. Start today, stay consistent, and diversify your revenue streams for the best chance at success.

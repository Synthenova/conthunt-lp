What Is an Instagram Reel? Complete Beginner’s Guide

Instagram Reels has become one of the most important features on the platform, offering creators a way to reach new audiences through short-form video content.

If you’re new to Reels or considering adding them to your content strategy, this guide explains everything you need to know.

Definition: What Is an Instagram Reel?

An Instagram Reel is a short-form vertical video that:

  • Can be up to 90 seconds in duration
  • Uses vertical 9:16 aspect ratio (full-screen on mobile)
  • Appears in the Reels tab and Explore page
  • Can include audio, effects, and text overlays
  • Is designed for mobile-first consumption

Key Difference: Reels are Instagram’s answer to TikTok—short, engaging videos meant to be discovered by new audiences, not just your followers.

How Instagram Reels Work

Where Reels Appear

Location Description
Reels Tab Dedicated section in Instagram app
Explore Page Discovered by non-followers
Profile Grid Saved to your profile
Stories Can be shared to Stories for 24 hours
Feed Occasionally appear in main feed

The Algorithm

Instagram’s Reels algorithm considers:

  • Watch time: How long viewers watch
  • Engagement: Likes, comments, shares, saves
  • Completion rate: How many watch to the end
  • Audio usage: Trending sounds boost reach
  • Relationship: Past interactions with creator

Reels vs. Other Instagram Content

Feature Reels Stories Posts
Duration Up to 90 seconds Up to 60 seconds Up to 60 minutes (video)
Permanence Permanent Disappear in 24 hours Permanent
Discovery High (Explore, Reels tab) Low (followers only) Medium
Editing Advanced tools Basic editing None in-app
Audio Music library Personal audio Personal audio

Key Insight: Reels are your best bet for reaching new audiences on Instagram.

Why Reels Matter for Creators

1. Reach Potential

Reels are Instagram’s primary growth engine. Unlike posts and Stories (mostly seen by followers), Reels can go viral and reach millions who don’t follow you.

2. Feature-Rich Creation Tools

Instagram provides:

  • Audio library: Trending sounds and music
  • Effects and filters: AR effects and visual enhancements
  • Editing tools: Trim, speed, timer, align
  • Text and stickers: Overlay text and graphics
  • Timer and countdown: Hands-free recording

3. Cross-Promotion

Reels can be:

  • Saved to your profile
  • Shared to Stories
  • Posted to feed
  • Shared to Facebook (if connected)

4. Engagement Boost

Reels typically generate more engagement than traditional posts because they’re:

  • More dynamic
  • Easier to consume
  • Favored by the algorithm

  • Day in the life
  • Travel content

How to Create an Instagram Reel

Step 1: Open Reels Camera

  • Open Instagram app
  • Tap + button or swipe right from feed
  • Select Reels at the bottom

Step 2: Choose Your Approach

Options:

  • Record new: Use Reels camera to record
  • Upload from gallery: Select existing video
  • Use templates: Start with trending format

Step 3: Add Elements

  • Audio: Choose from Instagram’s library
  • Effects: Add AR effects or filters
  • Text: Overlay text with timing controls
  • Stickers: Add interactive elements
  • Draw: Write or draw on video

Step 4: Edit Your Reel

  • Trim clips to desired length
  • Adjust speed (slow down or speed up)
  • Align clips for smooth transitions
  • Add timer for hands-free recording

Step 5: Share

  • Write caption
  • Add cover image
  • Tag accounts (if relevant)
  • Share to feed (optional)
  • Share to Stories (optional)
  • Tap Share

Video Specifications

Specification Requirement
Duration Up to 90 seconds
Aspect Ratio 9:16 (vertical)
Resolution 1080x1920 recommended
Format MP4 or MOV
File Size Under 4 GB

Content Guidelines

Reels must follow Instagram’s community guidelines:

  • No nudity or sexually explicit content
  • No hate speech or harassment
  • No dangerous activities or organizations
  • No misinformation (COVID-19, elections, etc.)

Audio in Reels

Using Trending Sounds

Trending audio is a major discovery factor:

  1. Tap the musical note icon
  2. Browse Trending section
  3. Select audio
  4. Record or upload your video

Why It Works: Instagram boosts Reels using trending sounds to more viewers.

Original Audio

You can also:

  • Use your own audio (voiceover, music you created)
  • Let others use your audio (enable in settings)
  • Credit original creators when remixing

Monetization

Instagram Bonus Program

Instagram periodically pays creators for Reels that meet certain criteria:

  • Original content (not reposted from TikTok)
  • Minimum view thresholds
  • Follow community guidelines

Note: The bonus program is invite-only and payouts vary.

Indirect Monetization

Most creators monetize Reels through:

  • Brand partnerships: Sponsored content
  • Product sales: Showcasing offerings
  • Service promotion: Driving to business
  • Affiliate marketing: Product recommendations
  • Funneling: Driving to other monetized platforms

Tips for Beginners

Start Simple

  • Use your smartphone
  • Film in good lighting (natural or ring light)
  • Keep Reels under 30 seconds
  • Focus on one clear idea

Use Trending Audio

  • Browse trending sounds regularly
  • Adapt trends to your niche
  • Jump on trends early (within 48 hours)
  • Credit original creators

Study Successful Reels

  • Follow creators in your niche
  • Note common patterns
  • Identify trending formats
  • Adapt to your unique style

Engage with Your Audience

  • Respond to comments
  • Use interactive stickers (polls, questions)
  • Ask viewers to share
  • Build community

Common Mistakes

Mistake 1: Ignoring Trending Audio

Problem: Missing out on discoverability.

Solution: Use trending audio when it fits your content.

Mistake 2: Poor Lighting

Problem: Grainy, unappealing video.

Solution: Film in good lighting or invest in a ring light.

Mistake 3: Weak Hooks

Problem: Boring first few seconds.

Solution: Start with action, movement, or compelling text.

Mistake 4: No Consistency

Problem: Posting sporadically.

Solution: Aim for 3-5 Reels per week.

Mistake 5: Reposting Without Adaptation

Problem: Direct reposts from TikTok with watermarks.

Solution: Create native content or remove watermarks.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Reels permanent?

Yes, Reels are saved to your profile and remain visible indefinitely (unless you delete them), unlike Stories which disappear after 24 hours.

Can anyone see my Reels?

Yes, Reels are public (unless your account is private). They can appear in the Reels tab and Explore page for potential new followers.

Do Reels perform better than posts?

Generally, yes. Reels receive more reach and engagement than traditional posts because Instagram actively promotes them in the algorithm.

Can I repost TikTok content to Reels?

Yes, but remove the TikTok watermark first. Native content (created in Instagram) typically performs better than reposted content.

Bottom Line

Instagram Reels is a powerful discovery and growth tool on the platform.

Key Takeaways:

  • Reels are vertical videos up to 90 seconds
  • Primary growth vehicle on Instagram
  • Advanced editing and audio features
  • Monetization through bonuses and partnerships
  • Cross-platform potential (TikTok, YouTube Shorts)

Getting Started:

  1. Open Instagram Reels camera
  2. Browse trending audio for inspiration
  3. Create simple, engaging content
  4. Post consistently (3-5 times per week)
  5. Engage with your audience

Cross-Platform Content Strategy

The Hub-and-Spoke Model

Hub (Long-form):

  • YouTube video
  • Blog post
  • Podcast episode
  • LinkedIn article

Spokes (Short-form):

  • YouTube Shorts snippets
  • TikTok adaptations
  • Instagram Reels versions
  • Twitter threads

Benefits:

  • Create once, distribute everywhere
  • Adapt for platform nuances
  • Drive traffic to hub content
  • Maximize ROI on creation

Content Adaptation Framework

Start with Core Concept: Example: “5 productivity tips for remote workers”

Platform Adaptations:

YouTube Shorts (60 seconds):

  • Hook: “Productivity hack that changed my life”
  • Quick tips 1-2 rapid fire
  • CTA: “Watch full video on my channel”

TikTok (15-60 seconds):

  • Trending sound
  • Text overlay with tips
  • Fast-paced editing
  • Caption: “Save for later”

Instagram Reels (30-60 seconds):

  • Aesthetic visuals
  • Text on screen
  • Multiple tips in carousel format
  • Engaging caption with more detail

LinkedIn (Image/Post):

  • Professional tone
  • Detailed explanation
  • Industry-specific context
  • Discussion prompt

Twitter/X:

  • Thread format
  • Each tip = separate tweet
  • Engaging final tweet with question

Multiply into 10+ pieces:

Organizing Your Content Ideas

The Content Bank System

Categories:

  • Quick wins (easy to create)
  • Deep dives (require research)
  • Trend adaptations (time-sensitive)
  • Series potential (multiple parts)
  • Collaborations (with others)
  • Seasonal content (specific timing)

Tracking Spreadsheet:

  • Content idea
  • Target platform(s)
  • Content format
  • Estimated effort
  • Priority level
  • Status (idea, in progress, published)

Weekly Review:

  • Select 3-5 ideas to create
  • Batch similar content
  • Maintain consistency
  • Track performance

Never Run Out of Content Ideas

The creators who never struggle with ideas have systems, not just creativity.

Build Your System:

  1. Collect ideas constantly (note app, spreadsheet)
  2. Categorize by platform and type
  3. Review weekly and select what to create
  4. Create in batches for efficiency
  5. Track performance to guide future ideas

How Much YouTube Shorts Pay for 1000 Views: 2026 RPM Guide

If you’re creating YouTube Shorts, one question inevitably comes up: How much money can I actually make? The answer isn’t straightforward, but understanding YouTube Shorts RPM (Revenue Per Mille, or per 1000 views) is essential for planning your content strategy.

Quick Answer: YouTube Shorts Pay Per 1000 Views

On average, YouTube Shorts pay between $0.01 and $0.06 per 1000 views. This translates to:

Views Estimated Earnings
1,000 $0.01 - $0.06
10,000 $0.10 - $0.60
100,000 $1 - $6
1,000,000 $10 - $60
10,000,000 $100 - $600

However, these numbers vary significantly based on your niche, audience location, and viewer engagement. Let’s dive deeper into what actually affects your earnings.

Why YouTube Shorts Pay Less Than Long-Form Videos

YouTube Shorts RPM is significantly lower than traditional long-form content, which typically pays $2-$10 per 1000 views. Here’s why:

  1. Ad Format Difference: Shorts use ads between videos in the feed, not pre-roll or mid-roll ads
  2. Lower Ad Demand: Short-form video inventory has historically commanded lower ad rates
  3. Revenue Sharing Model: YouTube shares 45% of Shorts ad revenue with creators (compared to 55% for long-form)
  4. User Intent: Viewers scrolling Shorts are less likely to engage with commercials

Despite lower RPM, Shorts offer a pathway to rapid audience growth that can lead to more lucrative long-form views and brand deals.

Factors That Affect Your YouTube Shorts Earnings

Several variables determine how much you’ll earn per 1000 views:

1. Audience Geography

Viewers from different countries generate different revenue:

Region RPM Multiple
United States, Canada, UK, Australia 3x - 5x base rate
Western Europe 2x - 3x base rate
Eastern Europe, Latin America 1x - 1.5x base rate
Asia, Africa, Southeast Asia 0.5x - 1x base rate

2. Viewer Age Demographics

Advertisers pay more for certain age groups:

  • 25-54 years old: Highest earnings (prime purchasing power)
  • 18-24 years old: Moderate earnings
  • 13-17 years old: Lower earnings (limited purchasing power)

3. Content Topic & Keywords

The topics you cover affect which ads appear:

  • High-value topics: Investing, real estate, insurance, software reviews
  • Mid-value topics: Tech tutorials, fitness, career advice
  • Lower-value topics: Gaming, memes, general entertainment

4. Seasonality

Ad rates fluctuate throughout the year:

  • Q4 (Oct-Dec): Highest RPM due to holiday shopping
  • Q1 (Jan-Mar): Lower RPM after holiday spend
  • Back-to-school (Aug-Sep): Education and tech RPM spikes

How to Calculate Your YouTube Shorts Earnings

Basic Formula:

Total Views ÷ 1000 × RPM = Earnings

Example Calculation:

500,000 views ÷ 1000 × $0.04 RPM = $20

Monthly Revenue Projection:

Daily Avg Views × 30 ÷ 1000 × Your RPM = Monthly Earnings

Ways to Increase Your YouTube Shorts RPM

While you can’t control the base RPM YouTube pays, you can optimize for higher earnings:

1. Target High-Value Niches

Create content in finance, tech, business, or educational topics where advertisers bid more aggressively.

2. Build a US/CAN/UK Audience

Create content that resonates with viewers in high-value geographic regions. Use trending topics relevant to these markets.

3. Use Strategic Keywords

Include high-value keywords in your titles and descriptions:

  • “How to save money on…”
  • “Best software for…”
  • “Investment tips for…”
  • “Business strategy for…”

4. Drive Long-Form Views

Use your Shorts to funnel viewers to your long-form videos where RPM is significantly higher. This is where the real money is made.

5. Diversify Income Streams

Don’t rely solely on ad revenue:

  • Brand sponsorships: $500-$5000+ per 100K followers
  • Affiliate marketing: Product recommendations in Shorts
  • Merchandise: Promote products in your content
  • Memberships: Offer exclusive content and perks

YouTube Partner Program Requirements for Shorts

To monetize your Shorts through the YouTube Partner Program:

  1. Get 10 million valid public Shorts views in 90 days
  2. Or meet the traditional thresholds (1000 subs + 4000 watch hours)
  3. Join YPP and connect your AdSense account
  4. Comply with all community guidelines

Once accepted, you’ll earn from both Shorts and long-form content on your channel.

Beyond Ad Revenue: The Real Money in Shorts

The most successful Shorts creators don’t rely on ad revenue alone. Here’s how they actually make money:

  1. Brand Deals: $1000-$10,000+ per sponsored Short
  2. Affiliate Marketing: Commissions on product recommendations
  3. Funnel to Long-Form: Shorts build audience for higher-RPM content

Frequently Asked Questions

Do YouTube Shorts pay the same as regular videos?

No. Shorts RPM ($0.01-$0.08) is much lower than long-form videos ($2-$10). However, Shorts can grow your audience faster.

How many views do you need on YouTube Shorts to get paid?

You need either 10 million public Shorts views in 90 days OR 1000 subscribers + 4000 hours of watch time to join the YouTube Partner Program.

Does re-uploading TikToks to YouTube Shorts make money?

Yes, if you own the content. However, original Shorts typically perform better and have higher monetization potential.

Can you make a living from YouTube Shorts?

It’s possible but difficult through ad revenue alone. Most full-time Shorts creators supplement with brand deals, sponsorships, and other income streams.

Bottom Line

YouTube Shorts pay between $0.01-$0.08 per 1000 views, with earnings varying by niche, audience location, and content type. While the direct ad revenue is modest, Shorts offer a powerful vehicle for rapid audience growth that can lead to more lucrative opportunities through brand deals, sponsorships, and long-form content monetization.

The key is to treat Shorts as part of a broader content strategy—use them to build your audience, then monetize through multiple revenue streams.

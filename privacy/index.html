Privacy Policy (conthunt)

Last Updated: January 13, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Synthenova Enterprises LLP (“Synthenova,” “Company,” “we,” “us,” “our”) collects, uses, discloses, stores, and protects information when you access or use conthunt (the “App,” “Service,” “Platform”) available at https://conthunt.app, including our websites, APIs, and related services.

This Privacy Policy is designed to meet common privacy notice expectations and align with applicable data protection principles (including India’s Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 and related Rules).

If you do not agree with this Privacy Policy, please do not use the Service.

1) Who We Are (Data Fiduciary / Controller)

Company Name: Synthenova Enterprises LLP Product: conthunt Address: Anna Nagar, Chennai 600040, India Email (Privacy & Support): support@conthunt.app

Depending on your location, Synthenova may be considered a “data fiduciary” (India) or “controller” (EU/UK-style frameworks), meaning we determine why and how your personal data is processed.

2) Scope: What This Policy Covers

This Privacy Policy applies to:

  • Visitors to our website(s)
  • Registered users of conthunt
  • Customers and prospective customers communicating with us
  • API users (if you access conthunt via API)
  • People whose personal data may appear within user-provided inputs (e.g., names contained in queries), where applicable

This policy does not cover third-party websites, social networks, or platforms you may access through our Service (e.g., YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, or other sources). Those platforms’ privacy policies apply to their services.

3) Key Definitions (Plain English)

  • Personal Data / Personal Information: information that identifies or can reasonably be linked to an individual (e.g., name, email, device identifiers, IP address, account IDs).
  • Processing: anything done with personal data (collecting, storing, using, sharing, deleting, etc.).
  • User Content / Inputs: the text, queries, URLs, prompts, filters, or other materials you submit to conthunt.
  • Third-Party Providers: vendors that help us operate the Service (e.g., cloud infrastructure, authentication, analytics, billing, and AI partners).

4) Information We Collect

We collect information in three main ways: (A) information you provide, (B) information collected automatically, and © information we receive from third parties.

4.1 Information You Provide to Us

Account & Profile Information

  • Name (if provided)
  • Email address
  • Login/auth identifiers (e.g., Firebase Auth user ID)
  • Organization/team info (if you add it)
  • Profile preferences and settings

Payment & Subscription Information

  • Subscription plan, billing cycle, invoices/receipts metadata
  • Payment status (paid, failed, refunded)
  • Limited payment identifiers from our payment processor (e.g., transaction IDs)
  • We generally do not store full card details on our servers; payments are handled by our payment processor(s).

Support & Communications

  • Emails, messages, and other content you send to support@conthunt.app
  • Bug reports, feedback, and form submissions
  • If you contact us, we may keep a record of that correspondence

Your Inputs to the Service

  • Search queries
  • Links/URLs to content you request to analyze or index
  • Filters, prompts, tags, and collections/boards you create
  • Outputs you generate (e.g., insights, summaries, metadata)
  • Note: Your inputs may include personal data if you choose to include it (for example, searching a person’s name).

4.2 Information We Collect Automatically

Device & Usage Data

  • IP address, device type, browser type, operating system
  • App/website usage events (e.g., features used, pages viewed)
  • Date/time stamps, referral URLs, clickstream, session information
  • Performance and diagnostic logs (crash logs, latency, error codes)

Cookies & Similar Technologies We may use cookies/local storage and similar technologies to:

  • Keep you logged in
  • Remember preferences
  • Measure performance and usage
  • Improve reliability and prevent abuse

You can control cookies through browser settings. Some features may not work properly if cookies are disabled.

4.3 Information We Receive From Third Parties

We may receive limited information from:

  • Authentication providers (e.g., Google/Firebase sign-in)
  • Payment processors (e.g., billing status, transaction confirmations)
  • Infrastructure and security providers (e.g., fraud signals, abuse prevention)
  • Data partners used to fetch public content metrics and metadata (where applicable)

5.2 Improve Performance, Quality, and Safety

5.3 Communications

  • Service-related notices (account, billing, security alerts)

  • Marketing communications (where allowed, and you can opt out)

5.4 Compliance and Legal Obligations

  • Enforce our Terms of Conditions
  • Comply with lawful requests, court orders, or applicable regulations
  • Protect rights, safety, and security of users, the public, and Synthenova

Your legal basis depends on where you are located and which laws apply.

6.1 India (DPDP Act)

Where India’s DPDP framework applies, we process personal data based on:

  • Consent, where required (which must be free, specific, informed, unambiguous, and withdrawable).
  • Certain “legitimate uses” (as recognized by law) for providing requested services, complying with legal obligations, and maintaining security.

We provide notice describing what personal data is collected and for what purpose, and we support consent withdrawal with comparable ease to granting consent.

6.2 GDPR-Style Frameworks (If Applicable)

Where the GDPR or similar laws apply, we may process personal data based on:

  • Contract necessity (to provide the Service you requested)
  • Legitimate interests (security, fraud prevention, service improvement)
  • Consent (where required, such as for certain marketing or cookies)

Individuals may have recognized data subject rights under GDPR-style laws. ([EDPB][1])

7.2 Legal, Safety, and Enforcement

We may disclose information if we believe it’s reasonably necessary to:

  • Comply with laws or lawful requests
  • Protect against fraud, abuse, or security threats
